Appetizers

Savory Puff Pastry Bites: Fill puff pastry with a variety of fillings such as spinach and feta, mushroom and garlic, or cream cheese and herbs.


Stuffed Breadsticks: Bake breadsticks filled with cheese, pesto, or olives. Serve with marinara sauce for dipping.


Bruschetta: Toast slices of baguette and top with a mixture of diced tomatoes, basil, garlic, and olive oil.


Vegetable Puffs: Create bite-sized pastries filled with minced vegetables and spices, perfect for dipping.

Main courses

Baked Pasta: Casseroles like lasagna or baked ziti, often made with homemade pasta or other baked goods.


Burek: A flaky pastry filled with meat, cheese, or spinach common in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisines.


Savory Crepes: Thin pancakes filled with ingredients such as ham, cheese, and spinach.


Calzones: Pizza dough stuffed with cheese, meats, and vegetables.

Desserts

Brownies: Fudgy or cakey, often made with chocolate, and sometimes topped with nuts or frosting.


Soufflés: Light, fluffy desserts that can be sweet or savory, with chocolate soufflé being a popular sweet option.


Macarons: Light and airy meringue cookies filled with ganache, buttercream, or jam.


Tarts: Shortcrust pastry filled with fruits, custards, or creams, such as fruit tarts or lemon tarts.

Desserts

Coffee: A popular choice, often served in various styles such as espresso, cappuccino, or drip coffee. It pairs well with pastries and breads.


Tea: Available in various types (black, green, herbal), tea provides a soothing option for customers looking for a lighter beverage to complement their baked goods.


Hot Chocolate: A rich and creamy beverage made from melted chocolate or cocoa powder, often topped with whipped cream or marshmallows, perfect for indulgent treats.


Fruit Juices: Freshly squeezed or bottled, options like orange, apple, or berry juices provide a sweet and tangy contrast to baked goods.
